## Recommendation From Linux Matrix

The Docker/Linux matrix supports these defaults for FORMAT-heavy BGZF filtering:

- BGZF auto cap: `6` worker threads.
- Predicate worker cap: keep `4` worker threads.
- Predicate batch records: `2048`.
- Queue batches: keep `2` as a stable compromise; stress was fastest at queue `1`, public CHM13 50k was fastest at queue `4`, and queue `2` stayed close while avoiding a workload-specific default.

Evidence summary:

- Stress 1M fastest row: queue `1`, batch `2048`, BGZF/filter `6:4`, `0.537784s`, RSS `8200 KB`.
- Stress 1M low-RSS near-fast row: queue `2`, batch `2048`, BGZF/filter `4:4`, `0.566883s`, RSS `6900 KB`.
- Public CHM13 50k fastest row: queue `4`, batch `2048`, BGZF/filter `6:4`, `0.837360s`, RSS `187752 KB`.
- Public CHM13 50k queue `2` near-fast row: queue `2`, batch `2048`, BGZF/filter `6:4`, `0.867390s`, RSS `188220 KB`.
- Batch `8192` consistently increased RSS, especially on public CHM13 50k where rows were around `620 MB`.
