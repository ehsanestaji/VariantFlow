\section{Benchmark evidence}

All reported measurements are tied to tracked benchmark reports containing
exact commands, competitor versions, correctness checks, caveats, and
reproducibility commands. VariantFlow compares primarily against bcftools
because it is the trusted performance and correctness baseline for many
VCF/BCF workflows. Rows in Table~\ref{tab:benchmark-summary} and
Figure~\ref{fig:speedup-ranges} are copied from the public release benchmark
table and source reports; no smoke-test row is used as a performance claim.

The strongest user-facing evidence is now public FORMAT-rich filtering. On the
DDBJ CHM13 human chr22 cohort with 3715 samples, bounded 1k/10k/50k rows for
DP, GQ, AD, selected-sample DP, and mixed QUAL+FORMAT predicates matched
bcftools core records and measured 4.74--17.78 times faster than
\texttt{bcftools filter}. These rows directly test the selective FORMAT
scanning design on a many-sample human VCF, while still avoiding a default full
27 GB remote cache.

Compressed public-data filtering provides a complementary I/O-heavy result. On
bounded IGSR chr22 public-heavy BGZF inputs, VariantFlow's default native BGZF
path matched bcftools core records and was 13.44--13.47 times faster than
\texttt{bcftools filter}. Conversely, on deterministic FORMAT-heavy stress
data, opt-in predicate parallelism matched bcftools core records and was
4.33--5.27 times faster than \texttt{bcftools filter}; this case exercises the
CPU side of selective FORMAT scanning.

Query-aware indexed filtering provides an additional high-skip result. In the
v24 public IGSR matrix, indexed output matched default native output
byte-for-byte and matched bcftools core records. The public \texttt{FILTER ==
"PASS"} rows measured 122.95--272.73 times faster than \texttt{bcftools}, while
\texttt{AF > 0.99} with 256-record chunks measured 4.70--4.93 times faster
through the requested 1M tier. Coarser chunks and low-skip predicates were
documented as fallbacks or non-wins, which is why indexed filtering is claimed
only for metadata-proven high-skip cases.

Population-genetics evidence addresses a different replacement question:
whether VariantFlow can match familiar VCFtools-style summaries on real cohort
data. On the 1000 Genomes/IGSR 3202-sample human biallelic cohort through the
925730-record requested 1M tier, normalized VCFtools parity passed for
supported diploid biallelic rows. VariantFlow measured 3.71 times faster for
frequency, 8.12 times faster for missingness, 1.15--1.49 times faster for
pi/Tajima summaries, 9.88 times faster for LD, and 1.37 times faster for
Weir-Cockerham Fst; HWE matched correctness but was slightly slower at 0.98x.

The columnar evidence supports a separate workflow claim. For repeated
analytical queries, VariantFlow exports selected columns to Parquet and then
uses DuckDB for repeated predicates and grouped summaries. In the measured v26
row-group/query matrix, DuckDB results over VariantFlow Parquet matched
normalized baselines and measured 1.19--19.20 times faster amortized and
2.51--240.27 times faster query-only on deterministic stress rows. This does
not mean Parquet replaces streaming VCF filters; it means export-once,
query-many workflows can avoid repeated decompression and parsing.
