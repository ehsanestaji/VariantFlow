\section{Benchmark evidence}

All measurements are tied to tracked benchmark reports with exact commands,
competitor versions, correctness checks, and caveats (Table~1).
VariantFlow reports a speedup only when the benchmark row records a
correctness comparison; rows that trail a competitor remain optimization
targets rather than claims.

On the DDBJ CHM13 human chr22 cohort (3\,715 samples), bounded FORMAT-rich
predicates matched bcftools core records at 4.74--17.78x. Query-aware
\texttt{.vfi} indexed filtering achieved 123--273x for high-skip
\texttt{FILTER == "PASS"} predicates where chunk metadata proves no record
can pass; low-skip predicates fall back to sequential scan.

Population-genetics summaries on the full IGSR 3\,202-sample biallelic cohort
show that selective execution scales to whole-chromosome data. Missingness
measured 4.78x faster than VCFtools on chr22 (1.07M records) and 3.67x on
chr1 (6.47M records), with constant 9~MB peak RSS regardless of chromosome
size. All population-genetics outputs were verified by byte-identical diff against
VCFtools~0.1.17 on the same inputs (Supplementary Table~S1); an independent
cross-check with scikit-allel confirmed numerical agreement.
LD measured 9.88x on a 925k-record tier. The Parquet export-once
workflow measured 1.19--19.20x amortized over repeated DuckDB queries.

VariantFlow implements the population-genetics statistics that are computable
in a single streaming pass over sites or windows---allele frequency,
missingness, heterozygosity, Hardy--Weinberg, $\pi$, missing-data-aware
$\pi$/$d_{XY}$, Tajima's $D$, the site-frequency spectrum, $F_{ST}$, and
LD (Figure~1). The site-frequency spectrum (\texttt{sfs})
reproduces scikit-allel's folded and unfolded spectra exactly. Statistics that
require the full genotype or haplotype matrix in memory---selection scans,
principal-component analysis, and $D$/$f$-statistics---are outside this
streaming scope and are deferred to scikit-allel; Supplementary Table~S5 maps
the full correspondence.
