\section{Benchmark evidence}

All reported measurements are tied to tracked benchmark reports containing
exact commands, competitor versions, correctness checks, caveats, and
reproducibility commands. VariantFlow compares primarily against bcftools
because it is the trusted performance and correctness baseline for many
VCF/BCF workflows.

\input{tables/benchmark_summary}

The strongest current evidence is for selective native streaming and
compressed public filtering. On bounded IGSR chr22 public-heavy BGZF inputs,
VariantFlow's default native BGZF path matched bcftools core records and was
13.44--13.47 times faster than \texttt{bcftools filter}. On deterministic
FORMAT-heavy stress data, opt-in predicate parallelism matched bcftools core
records and was 4.33--5.27 times faster than \texttt{bcftools filter}. For
repeated analytical workflows, exporting once to Parquet and querying with
DuckDB matched normalized bcftools baselines and reduced amortized runtime by
3.18--25.67 times in the measured workflows.
