\section{Introduction}

Post-calling variant analysis commonly involves repeated scans of large VCF,
BGZF-compressed VCF, or BCF files to apply quality filters, evaluate INFO and
FORMAT predicates, compute summaries, compare call sets, or export selected
columns for downstream analysis. VCF is an effective exchange format, but many
workflows only need a small subset of fields from each record. Parsing unused
INFO, FORMAT, and sample values can therefore become unnecessary work,
especially in large cohort or repeated-query settings.

Mature tools such as bcftools and HTSlib provide the compatibility baseline for
VCF/BCF streaming, BGZF compression, region access, filtering, querying, and
statistics \citep{bcftools,htslib}. VariantFlow is designed to complement this
ecosystem rather than replace it universally. Its central idea is selective
execution: for supported operations, parse only the fields required by the
current predicate or projection, preserve original records when possible, and
track every performance claim against correctness-matched benchmarks.
