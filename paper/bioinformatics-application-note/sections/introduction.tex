\section{Introduction}

Variant call format (VCF) files remain the lingua franca of post-calling
genomics workflows. They are easy to exchange, compress, index, and inspect,
but they are not always the most efficient execution layout for every
downstream task. A typical analysis may scan the same BGZF-compressed cohort VCF
many times to apply quality filters, evaluate INFO or FORMAT predicates,
summarize call sets, compare two files, and export selected columns for
statistical analysis. In many of these steps, the operation depends on only a
few fields such as QUAL, FILTER, INFO/DP, INFO/AF, or one FORMAT key. Parsing
unused INFO annotations, FORMAT vectors, and thousands of sample values can
therefore dominate runtime without changing the result.

Mature tools such as bcftools and HTSlib provide the compatibility baseline for
VCF/BCF streaming, BGZF compression, region access, filtering, querying, and
statistics \citep{bcftools,htslib}. Their breadth is essential: real projects
need BCF, tabix/CSI indexing, complex expressions, normalization, and durable
interoperability. VariantFlow is designed to complement this ecosystem rather
than replace it universally. Its central idea is selective execution: for
supported operations, parse only the fields required by the current predicate or
projection, preserve original records when possible, and track every
performance claim against correctness-matched benchmarks. This evidence-first
position is important for bioinformatics practice, where a speedup is useful
only when the output remains trustworthy and the limitations are visible.
